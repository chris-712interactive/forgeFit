### Important reality check

- **There is no master list of "all" legitimate grants.** Thousands exist across federal, state, corporate, and foundation sources. This playbook covers **verified, high-fit programs** for a U.S. software/fitness startup — not every grant in America.
- **The SBA does not give grants to start or grow a business.** [sba.gov/funding-programs/grants](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/grants) confirms this. Federal non-dilutive funding for tech companies flows primarily through **SBIR/STTR** (R&D grants, not marketing grants).
- **Most private grants fund general small business growth**, not SaaS customer acquisition specifically. Frame ForgeRep as a community-impact, health/wellness, or innovation story — and specify that grant dollars go to **marketing, creator partnerships, and conversion optimization** to reach paying customers.
- **Avoid scams:** Never pay large "application fees," never trust guaranteed funding, verify every program on its official domain. Legitimate grants do not require wire transfers or crypto.

##### Executive Summary *(993 characters)*

ForgeRep is a mobile fitness app that puts evidence-based strength training, nutrition tracking, and progress forecasting in one place — and it works when your gym has no cell signal.

Most lifters today juggle two or three apps: one to log workouts, one to track macros, and maybe a spreadsheet to see if they're actually on pace for their goals. "AI workout" apps generate plans nobody can explain. ForgeRep is different: every progression rule traces to peer-reviewed research you can tap and read. The app installs like a native app on your phone (no App Store required), logs sets offline, and syncs when you're back online.

We're launching publicly in Q3 2026 with a free tier that delivers a complete training loop and paid tiers ($8.99–$14.99/month) for analytics, community accountability, and wearable sync. The product is built, billing is live, and prize funding accelerates our path to 500 paying subscribers — proving that trust and retention, not hype, win in fitness software.

##### Problem *(659 characters)*

Serious recreational lifters—training 3–5 days/week with goals in both strength and nutrition—are stuck with fragmented, unreliable tools.

~25M English-speaking lifters track macros and follow programs, yet no mainstream app unifies both. Users juggle MyFitnessPal (nutrition), Strong/Hevy (logging), and spreadsheets—context is lost, adherence drops. Gyms routinely have poor connectivity; cloud-only apps fail mid-workout. "AI workout" apps can't explain their logic. Shame-based UX punishes missed days and drives returning lifters away.

This is a $10B+ market with $8–15/month willingness to pay and chronic retention problems incumbents haven't solved.

##### Solution and Value Proposition *(857 characters)*

ForgeRep is a mobile-first app that unifies evidence-based strength programming, macro tracking, progress projections, and community accountability—and works offline in the gym.

Four pillars: (1) citation-backed program engine (30+ peer-reviewed sources, not opaque AI); (2) offline set logging with reliable sync; (3) integrated nutrition diary and forecasts; (4) Pro-tier crews and leagues for retention.

Target market: adults 25–45 training 3–5×/week who've used MyFitnessPal plus a logging app and want one system. SAM: ~25M serious English-speaking lifters.

Vs. MyFitnessPal: we add programming. Vs. Strong/Hevy: we add macros and evidence. Vs. Fitbod: transparent citations, not black-box AI. Vs. MacroFactor: offline-first PWA plus community.

We win on trust and retention: offline reliability, evidence transparency, unified loop, shame-free UX.

NSF SBIR is **not a marketing grant**. You must propose **R&D with technical risk**, not "customer acquisition." Frame ForgeRep as:

> **Research question:** Can a deterministic, evidence-knowledge-base-driven programming engine — combined with offline adherence capture and integrated nutritional feedback — improve resistance training adherence and goal attainment vs. generic digital logging tools?

#### Project Pitch (NSF first step) — curated text

**Technology innovation (≤500 words summary)**

> ForgeRep proposes to develop and validate an Evidence-Based Physical Activity Prescription Engine (EPAPE) that generates periodized resistance training programs from a structured peer-reviewed knowledge base (YAML rules + citations), adapts loads/volume based on logged performance, and integrates macronutrient adherence signals — all operating offline-first via PWA/IndexedDB sync.
>
> **Technical risks:** (1) Validating that deterministic rule engines outperform LLM-generated programs on adherence and injury-safe progression; (2) Offline sync integrity under intermittent connectivity in gym environments; (3) Novel integration of performance + nutrition signals into unified projection models for body composition goals.
>
> **Differentiation:** Unlike Fitbod/AI generators (opaque) or Strong/Hevy (logging-only), EPAPE traces every progression decision to citable evidence rules — enabling both user trust and clinical/research auditability.

**Broader impacts**

> 80% of U.S. adults don't meet activity guidelines. Digital fitness tools reach millions but lack evidence transparency. Success would demonstrate a scalable model for evidence-linked physical activity prescription in consumer software — with open methodology suitable for academic collaboration.

**Commercial potential**

> Freemium SaaS ($8.99–$14.99/mo). SAM: ~25M serious English-speaking lifters. Phase I validates adherence lift; Phase II scales clinical partnerships and wearable integrations. No dilution — NSF takes zero equity.
